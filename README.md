# 👶 Games for My Daughter

A simple, beautiful site with fun and developmental games for babies 6–12 months old.

**Live site:** https://muhtarclaw.github.io/games-for-my-daughter/

---

## Activities

### 🪄 Peekaboo — Motor Skills
Hide behind a blanket or your hands and surprise your baby with a joyful "Peekaboo!" They'll love the anticipation and the happy reveal.

### 🧱 Stacking Cups & Blocks — Motor Skills
Give your baby chunky plastic cups, soft blocks, or nesting toys. They'll love knocking them down and rebuilding (with your help at first!).

### 🪑 Pull-to-Stand Practice — Motor Skills
Place sturdy furniture at a safe height for your baby to pull themselves up. Stay close and pad sharp corners!

### 🖐️ Texture Boards — Sensory Play
Create a board with different fabric swatches, sandpaper, bubble wrap, smooth wood, and more. Let your baby explore every texture!

### 💧 Water Play — Sensory Play
A shallow tub with warm water and cups, ladles, and floating toys. They'll pour, splash, and discover which objects sink or float.

### 🥁 Musical Instruments — Sensory Play
Pots, wooden spoons, a small xylophone, or shakers made from containers filled with rice or beans.

### 📖 Picture Books — Language & Social
Thick board books with bright colors and simple pictures. Let them turn the pages themselves!

### ⚽ Ball Rolling — Language & Social
Sit facing your baby and roll a soft ball back and forth. A perfect first lesson in turn-taking!

### 👏 Copycat Games — Language & Social
Clapping, pat-a-cake, waving, and blowing raspberries. Your baby will try to imitate you!

### 📦 Cardboard Boxes — Independent Play
Empty tissue boxes, cereal boxes, and shipping cartons are endlessly fascinating. Put things in, dump them out!

### 🔷 Shape Sorters — Independent Play
Simple shape-sorter toys with large holes and chunky pieces. Learn through trial and error!

### 🧩 Chunky Puzzles — Independent Play
Puzzles with large knobs for easy grabbing. Pegged pieces are perfect for small hands!

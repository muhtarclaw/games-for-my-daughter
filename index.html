👶 Games for My Daughter

Fun & developmental activities for babies 6–12 months

Motor Skills
🪄 Peekaboo
6+ months

Hide behind a blanket, towel, or your hands and surprise your baby with a joyful "Peekaboo!" They'll love the anticipation and the happy reveal.

💡 Use different voices for each peek — high, low, silly — to keep it exciting!
Motor Skills
🧱 Stacking Cups & Blocks
7+ months

Give your baby chunky plastic cups, soft blocks, or nesting toys. They'll love knocking them down and rebuilding (with your help at first!).

💡 Start with 3–4 pieces and add more as they get the hang of it.
Motor Skills
🪑 Pull-to-Stand Practice
8+ months

Place sturdy furniture (coffee table, couch) at a safe height for your baby to pull themselves up. Stay close for supervision!

💡 Pad sharp corners and anchor furniture to the wall for safety.
Sensory Play
🖐️ Texture Boards
6+ months

Create a board with different fabric swatches, sandpaper, bubble wrap, smooth wood, and more. Let your baby explore every texture!

💡 Use a small notebook to note which textures they reach for most — it reveals their preferences.
Sensory Play
💧 Water Play
8+ months

A shallow tub with warm water and cups, ladles, and floating toys. They'll pour, splash, and discover which objects sink or float.

💡 Always supervise closely. Add a few drops of food-safe dye for extra visual fun!
Sensory Play
🥁 Musical Instruments
7+ months

Pots, wooden spoons, a small xylophone, or shakers made from containers filled with rice or beans. Music + rhythm = pure joy.

💡 Make shakers together — it's a craft and a toy in one!
Language & Social
📖 Picture Books
6+ months

Thick board books with bright colors and simple pictures. Let them turn the pages themselves — even if it's 3 at once!

💡 Narrate everything: "Look, a red ball!" — building vocabulary starts early.
Language & Social
⚽ Ball Rolling
7+ months

Sit facing your baby a arm's length apart and roll a soft ball back and forth. A perfect first lesson in turn-taking!

💡 Use a lightweight foam ball — soft enough even if they bonk their own face.
Language & Social
👏 Copycat Games
7+ months

Clapping, pat-a-cake, waving, and blowing raspberries. Your baby will try to imitate you — and giggle at the results!

💡 Slow down your movements and repeat the same gestures. Repetition is the key.
Independent Play
📦 Cardboard Boxes
6+ months

Empty tissue boxes, cereal boxes, and shipping cartons are endlessly fascinating. Put things in, dump them out, crawl in — pure wonder.

💡 Keep a stash of clean boxes in a closet and rotate them every few days for "new" discovery.
Independent Play
🔷 Shape Sorters
9+ months

Simple shape-sorter toys with large holes and chunky pieces. They'll learn through trial and error — and celebrate when it clicks!

💡 Start with just 2 shapes. Too many at once is frustrating, not fun.
Independent Play
🧩 Chunky Puzzles
9+ months

Puzzles with large knobs for easy grabbing. Pegged pieces are perfect for small hands that haven't mastered the pincer grip yet.

💡 Choose 2–3 piece puzzles to start. Matching a picture = instant pride!
